Community Archiving Event

As part of a collaborative effort with fellow librarians, I co-led a community archiving initiative at our local neighborhood library, titled "Germantown Community Archiving: Let's Make Histories." Designed to encourage storytelling and the preservation of personal and community histories, the event brought together historical organizations from Germantown, Philadelphia, who shared artifacts and curated displays. Community members were invited to digitize personal documents and explore archival microfilm for research. Foundations Data & Information

This course focused on understanding data, information, and digital content. It also covered strategies for managing information within back-end systems to support front-end interfaces, while examining the societal and economic impacts of data. Additionally, the course explored the structuring and classification of information, including the influence of artificial intelligence. As part of the coursework, I conducted a systems comparison between two companies, analyzing their data collection and utilization processes.
