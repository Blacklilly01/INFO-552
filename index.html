About

My name is Najla Rashad, and I am currently pursuing a Master of Library and Information Science (MLIS) degree at Drexel University. Through my coursework and field experiences, I am building a strong foundation in librarianship and information science. I also serve as a Library Trainee at the Free Library of Philadelphia, where I gain hands-on experience in public service, community engagement, and daily library operations. I am deeply committed to equitable access to information, digital archiving, and the evolving role of libraries as inclusive, community-centered spaces. My academic and professional journey has been shaped by meaningful projects, specialized training, and real-world practice that align with these core values.

Community Archiving Event

As part of a collaborative effort with fellow librarians, I co-led a community archiving initiative at our local neighborhood library, titled "Germantown Community Archiving: Let's Make Histories." Designed to encourage storytelling and the preservation of personal and community histories, the event brought together historical organizations from Germantown, Philadelphia, who shared artifacts and curated displays. Community members were invited to digitize personal documents and explore archival microfilm for research. The initiative was featured in Drexel University’s College of Computing & Informatics (CCI) publication and stands as a highlight in my commitment to accessible, community-driven historical preservation.

Associations

American Library Association (ALA)

I am a student member of the American Library Association (ALA), the largest librarian organization in the world, dating back to the 1800s. ALA advocates for librarians and libraries, and promotes the rights to education, literacy, and intellectual freedom.

Visit ALA

Association for Women in Science (AWIS)

I am a member of the Association for Women in Science (AWIS), a national organization with a local Philadelphia chapter. AWIS advocates for systemic change to ensure that all women in science and STEM-related fields can achieve their full potential. Their work focuses on equity in the workplace and the recognition of women’s scientific and leadership achievements.

Pennsylvania Black Librarians Caucus (PaBLC)

I am a student member of the Pennsylvania Black Librarians Caucus (PaBLC), established in 2020. PaBLC's mission is to grow and strengthen the profession of librarianship by promoting education, career development, and recruitment efforts within Black communities.

Visit PaBLC

Black Caucus of the American Library Association (BCALA)

I am a new member of the Black Caucus of the American Library Association (BCALA). For over 50 years, BCALA has been advocating for the development, promotion, and improvement of library services and resources for African American communities across the United States, while also supporting the professional development of African American librarians.
